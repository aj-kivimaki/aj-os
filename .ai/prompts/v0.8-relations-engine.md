# Architectural Goal

AJ-OS currently treats every business module as an independent database.

This milestone introduces relationships between business modules.

The goal is **not** to synchronize Notion relations yet.

The goal is to represent relationships inside the AJ-OS Schema Engine.

Business relationships become part of the application model before they become part of the Notion implementation.

---

# Your Role

You are a senior TypeScript engineer extending the AJ-OS Schema Engine.

Reuse the existing architecture.

Avoid unnecessary abstractions.

Keep the implementation consistent with the existing codebase.

---

# Objective

Introduce a strongly typed Relation Definition that allows business modules to declare relationships with one another.

The Schema Engine should understand relations even if the Notion Translation Layer ignores them for now.

This milestone establishes the domain model only.

---

# Business Context

Business modules are no longer isolated.

Examples include:

Projects belong to one or more clients.

Clients (CRM contacts) may participate in multiple projects.

Future modules will also connect:

- Portfolio ↔ Projects
- Production Music ↔ Projects
- Finance ↔ Projects
- Dashboard ↔ Everything

AJ-OS should model these relationships independently from Notion.

---

# Requirements

Extend the Schema Engine.

Create:

```
src/schema/

relation.ts
```

Introduce a reusable RelationDefinition.

Suggested fields:

- relation name
- target database
- relation type

Support relation types:

- one_to_one
- one_to_many
- many_to_many

Extend DatabaseDefinition so business modules can declare relations.

Example:

Projects

↓

Client

↓

CRM

The Schema Engine should validate relation definitions.

# Business Modules

Update the Projects module.

Projects should declare a relationship to CRM.

Example:

```

Client

↓

```

Do not modify CRM beyond exposing whatever metadata is required.

---

# Translation Layer

Do NOT translate relations into Notion properties yet.

If relation definitions are encountered they should simply be ignored.

This is intentional.

The domain model comes first.

Infrastructure support comes later.

---

# Workspace Synchronization

Workspace synchronization should continue working exactly as before.

Projects and CRM should still synchronize successfully.

Relations must not break synchronization.

---

# Constraints

- Notion relation properties
- Rollups
- Schema updates
- Relation synchronization

- Cross-database lookups
