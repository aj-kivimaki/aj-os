# Goal

Define the deterministic Selection Policy used by the Selection Engine.

The Selection Policy specifies how collected knowledge is evaluated, prioritized, filtered, ordered, and when exact duplicate KnowledgeItems are eliminated.

This task defines only the deterministic policy.

It does not execute selection, integrate with the Context Builder, assemble Context Packages, or introduce behaviour.

# Purpose

The Selection Policy defines the deterministic platform rules governing knowledge selection.

The policy determines:

- which KnowledgeItems continue
- which KnowledgeItems are excluded
- the canonical deterministic ordering of selected KnowledgeItems, via an ordered comparator chain
- when exact duplicate KnowledgeItems are removed

The policy never modifies KnowledgeItems.

---

# Inputs

The Selection Policy evaluates immutable KnowledgeItems contained within a CollectionResult.

The policy does not communicate with providers.

The policy does not execute pipeline stages.

---

# Outputs

Deterministic selection decisions suitable for Selection Engine execution.

The policy itself does not construct a SelectionResult.

---

# Design Principles

## Deterministic

Identical inputs always produce identical decisions.

---

## Reproducible

Selection decisions must be reproducible across executions.

---

## Ordered Comparator Chain

Ordering is expressed as an ordered comparator chain, not as scores or numeric priorities.

Each comparator is deterministic. The chain terminates with an immutable platform identifier (for example `KnowledgeItem.id`) to guarantee a stable total ordering of selected KnowledgeItems.

The policy introduces no scoring algorithms, no numeric priority values, and no business-specific ranking heuristics. It specifies deterministic guarantees, not ranking heuristics.

The resulting canonical ordering is the platform guarantee; SelectionResult exposes no explicit priority field.

---

## Platform-Owned

Selection decisions belong exclusively to the platform.

Providers never determine selection priority.

---

## Preserve Knowledge Identity

KnowledgeItems are never rewritten, merged, summarized, or transformed.

---

## Exact Duplicate Elimination

Only exact duplicate KnowledgeItems may be eliminated.

Knowledge consolidation is explicitly out of scope.

---

## Independent Evaluation

KnowledgeItems are evaluated using deterministic platform policy.

Selection policy does not depend on runtime state.

# Exact Duplicate Definition

> **Status:** Approved (2026-07-09). This is the platform definition of "exact
> duplicate" referenced by CB-015, CB-016 and CB-018.

Two KnowledgeItems are exact duplicates **if and only if**:

- their `content` values are identical, **and**
- their entire `source` objects (`id`, `type`, `title`, `locator`) are structurally
  identical.

`KnowledgeItem.id` is **explicitly excluded** from duplicate identity.
`KnowledgeItem.id` exists solely as the immutable deterministic ordering
tie-breaker and must never participate in duplicate identity.

No normalization or transformation is applied. "Exact" means **literal structural
equality**.

When duplicate elimination occurs, the **first occurrence in canonical Selection
Policy order is retained** and every subsequent duplicate is moved to
`excludedItems`.

# Worklog Summary

_2026-07-09_

Implemented the deterministic **Selection Policy** (CB-015) as executable platform
behaviour — pure, stateless, identity-preserving functions in a new internal
`src/context-builder/selection/policy/` module:

- **`evaluation.ts`** — `evaluateKnowledgeItem(item)`, a deterministic per-item
  eligibility predicate. M3 rule: an item is eligible iff it carries knowledge
  (non-empty `content`). Profile-agnostic; no scoring, no business heuristic.
- **`filtering.ts`** — `isRetainedKnowledgeItem(item)`, the retention predicate the
  Selection Engine applies to partition items (retained iff eligible). Non-retained
  items are carried into `excludedItems` unchanged.
- **`comparators.ts`** — the ordered comparator chain `selectionComparatorChain`
  composed by `compareKnowledgeItems(a, b)`, terminating in `compareById` (immutable
  `KnowledgeItem.id`, compared by UTF-16 code unit — not `localeCompare` — for
  determinism). At M3 the chain holds only its terminal comparator; future profile
  comparators (M5) are prepended without changing the structure.
- **`index.ts`** — internal barrel. Deliberately **not** re-exported from
  `selection/index.ts` or `context-builder/index.ts`: ordering is the public
  guarantee, comparators are internal platform behaviour (mirroring how the CB-010
  `collectKnowledge` behaviour stays internal). CB-016 imports it to *apply* the
  policy.

The policy modifies no frozen contract (`SelectionResult`, `CollectionResult`,
Collection Engine, Provider Registry untouched), constructs no `SelectionResult`,
executes no engine, and exposes no priority/score/ranking value.

**Exact-duplicate elimination — deferred, then implemented after approval.** The
frozen plan listed it but gave no operative definition of "exact duplicate" — only a
negative constraint ("must not rely on `KnowledgeItem.id`") and an instruction to
define it first. Per the guardrail the definition was **not invented**; a Contract
Change Proposal was raised. It was **approved (2026-07-09)** and the smallest
planning correction applied — the **Exact Duplicate Definition** section was added to
this task, with one-line references in CB-016 and CB-018. Elimination is now
implemented in `duplicates.ts`:

- **`duplicates.ts`** — `isExactDuplicate(a, b)` (identical `content` +
  structurally identical `source`; `KnowledgeItem.id` excluded; literal equality, no
  normalization) and `partitionExactDuplicates(orderedItems)` →
  `{ retained, duplicates }` (retain the first occurrence in canonical order; every
  subsequent duplicate is destined for `excludedItems`). Comparison is
  field-by-field, not a serialized key, so no encoding choice can merge distinct
  items. Items are returned unchanged (identity preserved) and no SelectionResult is
  constructed — CB-016 applies it.

Validation: `npm run typecheck`, `npm test` (135 passing across 11 files —
unchanged; CB-015 adds no behaviour tests, per scope) and `npm run build` (emits
`dist/context-builder/selection/policy/`, including `duplicates`) — all green. No
frozen M1/M2/M3 platform contract changed; the only planning change is the approved
definition clarification.

# Lessons Learned

- **A negative constraint is not a definition.** The plan bounded "exact duplicate"
  (must not use `KnowledgeItem.id`) without defining it. A bound on a decision is not
  the decision — the disciplined move is to escalate the missing definition and
  implement the parts that *are* defined, not to let a plausible constraint
  masquerade as a specification.
- **Expose the guarantee, not the mechanism (continued from CB-014).** Keeping the
  comparator chain internal and publishing only the ordered `selectedItems` keeps a
  Policy implementation detail out of the public surface.
- **Deterministic string ordering must avoid `localeCompare`.** Locale-aware
  collation is environment-dependent; code-unit comparison keeps identifier ordering
  reproducible.

# Notes

The Selection Policy defines the deterministic decision-making model of the Context Builder.

Selection execution is intentionally deferred to CB-016.

This separation allows the policy to be reviewed, tested, and evolved independently of the Selection Engine implementation.

Implementation note: The platform meaning of "exact duplicate" is defined in the
**Exact Duplicate Definition** section above (approved 2026-07-09). As specified
there, the definition never relies on `KnowledgeItem.id` — the identifier serves as
the deterministic tie-breaker, not the duplicate identity.
