### RC-1 — Construction vs. Validation

Accepted.

The architecture now distinguishes two separate concerns:

### RC-2 — Explainability and Summary

Accepted.

The exclusion is clarified.

Assembly does **not compute explainability or summary** in Milestone 4.

The exclusion therefore applies to computed rationale, not to the existence of the required fields.

### RC-3 — Determinism and generated_at

Accepted.

The architecture is corrected to preserve Determinism by Construction.

Variable metadata such as `generated_at` is treated as an injected input rather than ambient state generated by Assembly.

Assembly therefore remains a deterministic, pure transformation from its inputs to the resulting ContextPackage.

### RC-4 — Section Classification Boundary

Accepted with clarification.

The architecture now explicitly constrains the future classification strategy:

- it must be deterministic,
- it must preserve frozen contracts,

Semantic classification is outside the responsibility of the Assembly stage.

AD-010 is clarified accordingly.

### RC-5 — Non-Knowledge-Derived Sections

Accepted.

The architecture now records that the completed ContextPackage is assembled from multiple deterministic inputs.

Selected KnowledgeItems are only one input source.

Sections such as Objective, Success Criteria, Constraints, and Open Questions require separate deterministic sources and are not assumed to originate from KnowledgeItems.

The architecture records this requirement without yet defining the mechanism.

---

### RC-6 — Order Preservation

Accepted.

The architecture now explicitly guarantees that any future section-classification strategy preserves the canonical ordering established by Selection.

Assembly may partition knowledge into sections, but it never reorders knowledge.

Canonical ordering remains part of the frozen contract.
